About

Deane Nettles is a graphic designer, photographer, and illustrator. He has an MFA in Integrated Design from the University of Baltimore and has taught at numerous colleges and universities in the DC/Baltimore area.

He began on a college newspaper, The Collective, first as a photographer and then as a layout artist. He received his undergraduate degree in news journalism, then went back for a degree in advertising design and returned to edit The Collective. His former editor got him a position working as a production artist at boutique agency Morgan Burchette; from there he became art director at KSK Communications.

Since then he has freelanced for ad agencies, design studios, direct marketing firms and publications. He has served as art director for several publications and was senior art director at August, Lang & Husak in Maryland.

He is a past president and life member of the Art Directors Club of Metropolitan Washington. Among other accomplishments was running the Annual Show for several years,produced a branding workshop conducted by Paula Scher of Pentagram, and facilitating a management workshop for the Board.

Deane started teaching in 2006 and has taught courses in design and web design and advanced courses in typography, advertising and branding.

Deane is always looking to work with an energetic, committed team like the one on his college newspaper — groups told to quiet down by the classes next door and happy to continue their enthusiastic arguments over pizzas after hours.
